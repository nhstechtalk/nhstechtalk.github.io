GOOGLE SCIENCE FAIR 2015

 

The Google Science Fair is an innovative global competition that challenges young minds all over the world to present their ideas, inventions, and advancements in any field of science. We will be working on this throughout the year, specifically focused on the technological aspect of the competition.

RASPBERRY PI & ARDUINO

 

The Raspberry Pi and Arduino are both small circuit boards with immense potential. These versatile tools are excellent for introducing interested individuals to programming and hardware. Some projects that these boards can be used for include home automation and smart homes (as seen at our local Solar Decathlon), wearable computers, arcade machines, robots, and even full-blown servers.

HACKATHONS

 

HACKATHONS. DUN DUN DUN.

To the uninitiated, the word "hackathon" bears a formidable title, no doubt rendering the image of a bunch of notorious troublemakers in a seedy, underground location trying to bring down the government. This is completely absurd, and only fits with Hollywood's skewed perception of the word "hacker". A hacker is someone who thinks outside the box. A hacker creates and innovates. Hackathons themselves are incredibly inviting. In fact, you don't even need to have touched code, you can learn while you're there! Hackathons are literally competitions of creation and innovations, with top tech companies such as Google, Intel, Amazon, Dell, and several others sponsoring and scouting talent. Hackathons we plan to attend this year include HackUCI, LAHacks, CodeDay, and HackTech. At our club, we will perpare you to atttend these hackathons if you would like to compete. Did we mention that there are prizes?

 

To find out more about hackathons, click the link before. The first link focuses on college hackathons in particular.
